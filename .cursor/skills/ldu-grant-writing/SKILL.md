## Key terminology (use exactly)
- **The Corridor**: Geographic/conceptual link between South LA (Crenshaw) and the farm
- **Cultivation Campus**: The NorCal farm property — never "camp"
- **Seasonal Exchange**: Structured farm residencies aligned with growing seasons
- **The Factory**: Clothing manufacturing at Cultivation Campus
- **The Supply Chain**: Closed-loop Studio WELEH → The Factory connection
- **La Collina philosophy**: The Cultivation Campus ethos

## Narrative structure (standard order)
1. **Executive Summary** — mission + ask in 2-3 sentences
2. **Organizational Background** — LDU history, demographics, Crenshaw context
3. **Program Narrative** — specific pillar program description, evidence of need
4. **Implementation Plan** — timeline, milestones, staff roles
5. **Evaluation** — how outcomes will be measured
6. **Budget Narrative** — line-item justification aligned to award ask
7. **Appendices** — 501c3 letter, financials, bios (if required)

## Voice guidelines
- Active, confident, never defensive
- Ground every claim in South LA context or lived experience
- Avoid corporate jargon — write how the community talks
- Mention intergenerational leadership (mother-daughter) when relevant
- When writing for government grants (CalRecycle, USDA, NEA): use formal policy language and cite statute numbers

## Scoring threshold reminder
- Score ≥ 3.5 → writing queue
- Pillar 5 (Founder) → 3.0+ threshold, fast turnaround
- Never write for grants under $5,000 unless Pillar 5 with strategic value
