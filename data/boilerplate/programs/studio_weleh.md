# Studio WELEH — Program Description Boilerplate

## Overview
Based in South Central Los Angeles, Studio WELEH is where art, apparel, fabrication, and storytelling come together under one roof. It is the physical home of When Everyone Loves, Everyone Heals — the philosophy and business that guides everything we build. The studio is an active working space where garments are designed, large-scale artworks are constructed, characters are developed, and new ideas are tested in real time. What happens inside the studio does not live in isolation. Each piece created, each collaboration formed, and each activation hosted is part of a larger connected system.

When Everyone Loves, Everyone Heals is about building structures that circulate opportunity. Through exhibitions, product releases, workshops, and cultural programs connected to Life Development University, Studio WELEH turns creativity into access — creating paid work, skill development, and shared experiences that strengthen the surrounding community. The art supports the apparel. The apparel supports the programming. The programming supports the people. The system works because each part reinforces the other.

The long-term direction is clear. From Studio to Soil. Studio WELEH is the starting point and the engine. The larger goal is to acquire land and build a permanent creative headquarters where art, education, fabrication, and agriculture coexist. Every garment sold, every artwork placed, and every partnership secured helps move that vision forward. It is a growing ecosystem designed to create lasting cultural and economic impact rooted in the belief that when everyone loves, everyone heals.

## Studio WELEH's Role Within LDU
Studio WELEH is an example of a business that LDU has incubated — one that now springs forth with community enrichment opportunities based on shared goals and resources provided through the LDU constellation. It is a model for entrepreneurial and artistic success, demonstrating cooperative economics in practice: building each other up to support the community as a whole.

Weleh Dennis is an Inglewood-based artist whose talents and creative practice are woven into LDU's experiential programming. He leads workshops, brings cultural activations to the community, and contributes his artistic vision to the campus environment.

The goal is to use Studio WELEH's arts programming as a vehicle for receiving grants centered around the arts and bringing creative opportunities to community members — from high school to college-aged youth — while facilitating creative space as an arena for artists within LDU to uplift the arts in California.

## SB 707 / CalRecycle Positioning
Studio WELEH's sustainable apparel programming teaches closed-loop design — collecting, cleaning, redesigning, and upcycling textiles. This positions LDU as a CalRecycle-ready textile diversion partner aligned with California SB 707 (Responsible Textile Recovery Act of 2024). Similar California-based textile recycling operations have received over $1.3 million from CalRecycle's Recycled Fiber, Plastic, and Glass Grant Program. LDU's model adds dimensions these operations typically lack: youth education, workforce development, and community-based collection infrastructure in an underserved neighborhood.
