# Textile Sustainability & SB 707 — Narrative Boilerplate

## SB 707 Overview
California Senate Bill 707 (Responsible Textile Recovery Act of 2024), signed September 2024, establishes the nation's first Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) textile recycling program. This creates a new ecosystem of grants for textile collection, sorting, repair, recycling, upcycling, and sustainability education.

## SB 707 Timeline
- January 2026: CalRecycle begins accepting PRO applications
- March 2026: CalRecycle selects the PRO
- July 2026: Producers must join the approved PRO
- July 2028: CalRecycle adopts implementing regulations
- July 2030: PRO stewardship plan activates — infrastructure grants flow

## LDU / Studio WELEH Positioning
Studio WELEH's sustainable apparel programming within LDU teaches closed-loop design: collecting, sorting, cleaning, redesigning, and upcycling textiles. This positions LDU as a CalRecycle-ready textile diversion partner aligned with SB 707.

Similar California-based textile recycling operations have received over $1.3 million from CalRecycle's Recycled Fiber, Plastic, and Glass Grant Program. LDU's model adds critical dimensions that most textile diversion operations lack: youth education and workforce development integrated with textile diversion, community-based collection infrastructure in an underserved South LA neighborhood, and re-entry employment pathways through textile sorting and upcycling.
