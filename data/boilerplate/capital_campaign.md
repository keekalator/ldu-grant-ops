# Capital Campaign — Narrative Boilerplate

## Overview
LDU's capital needs span two distinct sites within the family's constellation of ventures: the existing campus on Crenshaw Boulevard and the Northern California land acquisition. Each has its own development trajectory, funding strategy, and execution timeline.

## Site 1: 4241 Crenshaw Boulevard (The Power Building)
The existing campus, acquired free and clear with a $3.3 million state allocation from Senator Steven Bradford. Currently operational as a state-of-the-art facility with modular storefront, makers lab, memory center, AI lab (in development), outdoor bar and kitchen, and tiered seating.

### 2026 Strategy: Heal While We Build
The current phase activates the campus as a venue — hosting brand partnerships, cultural experiences, and community events that generate the revenue needed to actualize the spatial renderings still requiring developmental budget. These activations bring exposure and experiences never before seen in the community, leveraging the facility's capabilities and emerging partnerships. In the first quarter launch alone, LDU welcomed over 1,000 community members. Data capture and direct community engagement during this phase inform which programs are best suited for the area.

### 2026-2027: Scaling to Full Educational Environment
Beginning August 2026, LDU transitions from venue to full-scale educational environment. This requires:
- Architectural plans for two-story campus expansion
- Design and engineering for the expanded facility
- Construction and buildout execution
- Equipment and infrastructure for workforce development programming (AI lab, vocational training, content production)
- Furnishing and outfitting educational spaces

The goal: a campus with the equipment and infrastructure to change lives — workforce development, AI and technology training, vocational skills, financial literacy, and wellness programming delivered at scale.

### Design Development: Perkins Rottinger (Spatial Design Partner)
LDU has engaged Perkins Rottinger for design development and architectural plans for the two-story expansion. Total design development cost: $40,000.

Phase 1 — Feasibility + Code Review (3 Weeks):
Existing building and site context 3D model, zoning and code analysis (height, setbacks, FAR, occupancy), ADA requirements, egress requirements and strategy, stair and elevator placement studies, preliminary structural feasibility assessment (capacity of existing structure and reinforcement strategy), initial spatial layout options integrating the proposed addition with the current building, and makers lab millwork drawings. Deliverable: feasibility summary outlining constraints, opportunities, recommended direction, and preliminary plan studies.

Phase 2 — Concept Design (6-8 Weeks):
3-4 two-week design rounds with bi-weekly reviews. Interior spatial concepts and layout development, facade design options and development, coordinated plans and renderings. Deliverable: comprehensive concept package including developed plans and approximately 8-10 renderings (interior and exterior views) communicating architectural intent and spatial organization.

Following Phase 2, a Schematic Design phase will coordinate with consultants, contractors, and an Architect of Record. That scope will be defined once Concept Design is complete.

## Site 2: Northern California Land Acquisition
Twenty to eighty acres in the Sacramento Valley agricultural corridor (Woodland/Winters, Yolo County). First-time land acquisition in partnership with Weleh Dennis. See programs/ag_extension.md for full land vision.

## Campus Revenue Model (Crenshaw)
LDU operates a tiered venue access model generating earned revenue during the activation phase. This revenue model is central to the organization's self-sufficiency — generating income to continue building, acquiring technology and equipment, and progressing the facility while sustaining operations independently of grant funding.

- Coworking: $50 / $100 / $250 tiers
- Blessings (community rate): Includes cleaning + 1 guard, social contract language
- Collaborator tier: For recurring programming partners
- Community tier: For larger community events
- Corporate Campus Takeover: Up to $20,000
- Add-ons: Catering, bar, AV, PAs, brand ambassadors, content capture
