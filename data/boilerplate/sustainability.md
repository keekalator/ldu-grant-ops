# Sustainability — Narrative Boilerplate

## Diversified Funding Strategy
LDU pursues a diversified funding model combining government grants, foundation support, corporate partnerships, earned revenue from venue operations and program fees, and individual giving. This multi-stream approach ensures no single funder represents more than 25% of operating revenue.

## Earned Revenue Streams
- Venue rental income (tiered model from $50 coworking to $20,000 corporate takeover)
- Brand activation and partnership fees
- Program fees for masterclasses and workshops (as programming launches)
- Community outreach and data capture services (currently contracted with LA County and Destination Crenshaw)
- Content capture and production services
- Add-on services (catering, bar, AV, staffing)

## Corporate Partnership Model
LDU offers brands authentic community engagement — not a logo placement, but genuine collaborative programming and cultural activations. The 2026 venue activation strategy is specifically designed to build these partnerships and generate the revenue needed to scale into full educational programming.

## SB 707 / Circular Economy Revenue
Studio WELEH's sustainable apparel programming positions LDU within California's emerging textile recycling economy. As SB 707 implementation progresses (PRO formation 2026, infrastructure grants 2028-2030), LDU is positioned as a collection, education, and sorting partner — creating a future revenue stream from the circular textile economy.

## Sustainability Narrative for Grants
LDU is not dependent on grant funding to exist. The campus is owned free and clear. The venue generates earned revenue. The founders' constellation of ventures provides operational stability and diverse income streams. Every event, partnership, and booking builds the infrastructure for what comes next — generating the income to continue acquiring technology, equipment, and infrastructure while sustaining operations independently. Grant funding accelerates programming capacity and capital improvements but does not sustain basic operations. This is a key differentiator for funders: you are investing in growth, not survival.
