## Mission Statement (Long — Federal/Major Applications, 250 words)
Life Development University (LDU) is a family-owned creative campus on Crenshaw Boulevard in South Los Angeles, operating as a program of Life Development Group 501(c)(3). Founded by "The Kikas" — mother-daughter duo Kika Keith (CEO) and Kika Howze (CCO) — LDU was built on the belief that cultural infrastructure should be owned and operated by the community it serves. The campus, acquired free and clear with a $3.3 million state allocation from Senator Steven Bradford, was fully renovated from a former predatory check-cashing facility into a learning environment where education and wellness live at the center of everything we do.

LDU currently houses a modular storefront space, a makers lab, a memory center, an AI lab (in development), outdoor bar and kitchen, and two-tiered seating. Programming includes family experiences, masterclasses, brand activations, and workforce development across creative fields, artificial intelligence, hospitality, and financial literacy. LDU is part of a broader constellation of interconnected ventures — including Studio WELEH, a South Central-based artist studio, and a growing vision for land acquisition in Northern California — each designed to expand the organization's capacity to develop people, places, and enterprises rooted in community.

LDU's revenue model is built for self-sufficiency. Every event, partnership, and booking generates the income needed to continue building — acquiring the technology, equipment, and infrastructure to progress the facility while sustaining operations independently of grant funding. This positions LDU not as an organization seeking survival funding, but as a self-sustaining institution pursuing growth capital. LDU is not a grant-funded pop-up or temporary activation. It is a permanent commitment to developing the people who call this corridor home.

## Organizational History
Life Development University exists because its founders understand what it takes to build something from nothing on the Crenshaw corridor. Kika Keith and Kika Howze — "The Kikas," a mother-daughter duo — are a family team with four generations of entrepreneurship behind them. Before LDU, they built a successful for-profit business on Crenshaw Boulevard, proving they could operate and sustain a community-facing enterprise in a historically underinvested neighborhood. That experience became the foundation for LDU: the business acumen, the community relationships, the operational discipline, and the conviction that ownership matters.

The Kikas acquired the building at 4241 Crenshaw Boulevard — a former predatory check-cashing facility — with a $3.3 million state budget allocation from Senator Steven Bradford. They completely renovated it into the creative campus that is now Life Development University. Where a predatory business once stood, there is now a state-of-the-art facility built for family wellness, resources, and education — not limited by age.

In 2026, LDU's strategy is to heal while we build. The campus is being activated as a venue — hosting experiences, brand partnerships, and cultural moments that generate the funds needed to actualize the spatial renderings still requiring developmental budget. These activations offer exposure and experiences never before seen in our community, made possible by our state-of-the-art facility and emerging partnerships. In our first quarter launch alone, LDU welcomed over 1,000 people through its doors. Data capture and direct communication with the community during this phase will show us which programs are best suited for our area and what our neighbors actually need.

Beginning in August 2026 and into 2027, LDU will transition from a venue into a full-scale educational environment with the equipment and infrastructure to change lives — workforce development, AI and technology training, vocational skills, financial literacy, and wellness programming. The goal is a two-story campus expansion, with architectural plans, design, and execution requiring additional capital investment.

## Leadership Bios

### Kika Keith — CEO, Life Development University
Kika Keith — known to most as Big Kika — is a serial entrepreneur, community advocate, and the CEO of Life Development University. Raised in Leimert Park since her family migrated from Lancaster, Pennsylvania in the early 1970s, she carries a legacy of ownership in her blood. Her grandfather owned the first Black-owned hotel and bar in Lancaster. Her mother was a Black Panther. Her parents were freedom fighters. She learned early that business was not just about profit — it was about power, dignity, and service.

Before LDU, she founded Sweet Strings, an inner-city youth orchestra with 300 youth enrolled and 1,000 more on the waiting list, and Gorilla Life, a chlorophyll beverage line that reached over 300 Whole Foods stores. She has spent decades building businesses that serve the community they come from. Now, with LDU, she is building the next layer of the vision. She often says, "When the system won't build with you, you build your own." The Power Building is proof.

### Kika Howze — CCO, Life Development University / CEO, Studio WELEH
Kika Howze is the CCO of Life Development University and the CEO of Studio WELEH, an artist studio she co-founded with artist and designer Weleh Dennis. With 15 years of experience across music, advertising, film, and multicultural marketing, she has worked with Usher Raymond's management office, Goodby Silverstein, Mother NY, and Team Epiphany — executing campaigns for Coca-Cola, Google, Nike, Heineken, W Hotels, HBO, and others.

At 17, she left LA for New York to work in talent management. Eleven years later, she came back home and poured everything she learned into building alongside her mother. She grew up in homes filled with the work of Charles White, David Hammons, and Betye Saar — and that eye for visual art now shapes every brand touchpoint, every campaign, and every creative decision across the family's constellation of ventures.
